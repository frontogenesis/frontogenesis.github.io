Forecast Summary

Updated January 25, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, particularly near the coast, expect challenging marine conditions starting Sunday morning as a cold front brings strong offshore winds and elevated seas of 4-7 feet, with a few gusts possibly nearing gale force. These conditions will persist through Monday night before easing on Tuesday. Temperatures along the coast will remain dangerously cold, especially at night and early morning through Tuesday, with wind chills dropping significantly. While significant icing is expected inland, coastal areas should see mainly liquid precipitation. Another cold front on Friday could bring more strong offshore winds and rough seas, with temperatures staying well below normal through the weekend.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect challenging marine conditions through Monday with strong north winds, reaching near gale force at times, especially Sunday night, under a Small Craft Advisory. Along the coast, temperatures will hover near freezing with wind chills feeling well below freezing through Monday morning. Light rain or drizzle is likely Sunday morning, diminishing by the afternoon. Highs will only reach the 40s on Sunday and Monday, with very cold nights dropping into the low to mid-20s. Conditions may briefly improve mid-week with onshore flow, but another cold front could bring strong winds again by the end of the week.

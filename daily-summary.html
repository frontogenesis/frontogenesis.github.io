Forecast Summary

Updated December 25, 2025

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect mild and warm weather near the coast through the end of Christmas week, with near-record high temperatures in the upper 70s. Light onshore winds will continue over the coastal waters through Sunday afternoon, but a strong offshore flow with gusty winds is likely late Sunday night into early Monday as a cold front moves through. Marine conditions may include dense fog over nearshore waters and coastal lakes and bays each night into the morning hours for the rest of the week, potentially reducing visibility. No precipitation is expected in the short term, but cooler weather will arrive early next week following the cold front.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect warm and humid weather near the coast through the weekend, with highs in the 80s on Christmas Day. Marine conditions will feature gentle to moderate onshore winds and occasional patchy to dense fog over northern nearshore waters and bays each night through Saturday. A few streamer showers are possible on Christmas morning, with a 10-15% chance of rain. A cold front arriving Sunday night into Monday will bring stronger northeast winds, potentially reaching gale strength, along with rough seas of 5-8 feet and a higher chance of rain (30-50%). Cooler temperatures are expected early next week following the front.

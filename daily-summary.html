Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect challenging marine conditions near the coast over the next few days. Strong offshore winds of 25-30 knots with gusts up to 35 knots are forecast through this morning, along with rough seas of 4 to 7 feet. Low water levels are also expected in coastal lakes and bays during low tide this morning. Winds and seas will ease this afternoon but will turn onshore by Friday, before another round of strong offshore winds arrives Friday night with a new cold front. On land near the coast, temperatures will be cool today with highs in the upper 50s, warming to the upper 60s or lower 70s on Friday. A cooler, drier airmass will move in over the weekend, with little chance of rain expected.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect hazardous marine conditions through Thursday afternoon, with strong northeasterly winds easing by evening. Winds will shift to a moderate southerly breeze on Friday, but a cold front late Friday into early Saturday will bring strong northeasterly winds again, likely leading to Small Craft Advisory conditions on Saturday. Along the coast, temperatures will cool to around 50 degrees Saturday night, with highs in the low to mid-60s over the weekend, warming slightly to the 70s by Monday. Rain chances are low to moderate (30-45%) on Saturday and again early next week, with possible rainfall totals near 0.50 inches along coastal areas, though timing and amounts remain uncertain.

Forecast Summary

Updated July 23, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: Tropical Storm Bertha will move west near the coast of Southwest Louisiana tonight and Thursday, bringing strong gusty winds, choppy waters, and possible strong currents to marine areas, with most impacts remaining offshore. Isolated heavy rains and a storm surge of 1 to 3 feet are possible near the coast through Thursday. Conditions should improve Thursday night. An upper ridge will then bring limited rain chances and above-normal temperatures from Friday onward.

Texas Coastal Bend: A Small Craft Advisory is in effect this afternoon through evening for the coastal waters near the Texas Coastal Bend as Tropical Storm Bertha approaches, with winds increasing to fresh to strong levels (Beaufort 5-6) and building swells and waves. Winds will start gentle to moderate southwesterly this morning before turning onshore and then settling to moderate for the rest of the period. Scattered to numerous showers and thunderstorms with locally heavy rain are expected Thursday afternoon and evening over the Gulf waters, accompanied by moderate to high rain chances into Friday. A Heat Advisory remains in effect today through 7 pm.

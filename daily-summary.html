Updated April 15, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect calm marine conditions near the coast through the end of the week, with steady onshore winds of 10 to 15 knots and waves under 5 feet. However, a cold front arriving Saturday evening will bring rain and a shift in winds, with strong offshore winds of around 30 knots and gusts up to 40 knots possible Sunday night into Monday, likely prompting marine warnings. Weather near the coast will be warm and humid through the week, with highs in the mid to upper 80s, before the front ushers in cooler, drier air on Sunday with highs dropping to the 70s. Rain and a few thunderstorms are expected with the front, though rainfall amounts will be light, between 0.25 to 0.5 inches.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect moderate to strong onshore winds over the marine zones, especially by Friday when advisory conditions are likely, along with a moderate risk of rip currents and potential minor coastal flooding increasing toward the weekend. Near the coast, anticipate very warm to hot temperatures through Saturday, with highs in the mid-90s inland, cooling significantly to the 70s or even 60s by Sunday and Monday. Dry conditions are forecast through Friday, but there’s a chance of light showers starting Saturday morning, with better rain chances (30-50%) Saturday night into Sunday as a cold front approaches, though rainfall amounts will likely be under half an inch.

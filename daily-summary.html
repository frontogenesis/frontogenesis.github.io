Forecast Summary

Updated February 03, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect mild temperatures today with showers and a few possible thunderstorms near the coast as a cold front approaches this afternoon into tonight, bringing rainfall totals of about a quarter to half an inch. Marine conditions will feature onshore winds of 10-15 knots today, shifting to offshore winds of 15-20 knots after the front passes on Wednesday through Thursday morning. Closer to the coast, temperatures will dip to the upper 30s by Thursday morning, with milder weather returning by the weekend.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect breezy conditions along the coast with strong winds tonight through Tuesday morning, prompting a Small Craft Advisory for marine areas until the afternoon. Winds will ease Tuesday but may pick up again overnight into Wednesday as a cold front moves through. A few showers are possible near the coast, mainly in the eastern areas, through Wednesday. Marine fog could develop on Tuesday, though this remains uncertain and depends on water temperatures. Overall, temperatures will warm through the week, reaching the 80s by the weekend, with generally dry conditions expected after midweek.

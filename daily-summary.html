Forecast Summary

Updated July 21, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: Tropical Storm Bertha is forecast to move westward near or just inland of the northern Gulf Coast through Friday, remaining asymmetrical with most impacts south of the center. Modest west-northwest winds are expected over the northwest Gulf into Wednesday, with marine conditions Wednesday night through Friday depending on the storm's exact track and strength; all interests should monitor updates. Near the coast, oppressive heat with afternoon heat indices of 107-115 is likely Tuesday into Wednesday, followed by a chance for locally heavy showers along the immediate coast Thursday before the system weakens. Hot conditions and limited rain chances are expected over the weekend.

Texas Coastal Bend: Dry conditions and hot weather will continue along the Texas Coastal Bend through at least midweek, with afternoon highs in the mid-90s to around 105 and heat indices peaking between 105-110. A gentle to moderate southerly wind will persist over coastal waters, with seas building to 3-5 feet by midweek. Tropical Storm Bertha is expected to track across the northern Gulf with only limited direct impacts, though building easterly swell and increasing seas are likely late in the week along with low to moderate shower chances.

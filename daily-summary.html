Forecast Summary

Updated June 29, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: A weak surface high will bring light to modest southerly winds and low seas to the coastal waters of Southwest Louisiana through the week. Hot and humid conditions will continue with afternoon highs in the low to mid 90s and heat indices of 100 to 107 degrees. Hazy skies from Saharan dust are expected into early Tuesday, followed by a return of mainly afternoon showers and thunderstorms near the coast and over the coastal lakes and bays by midweek.

Texas Coastal Bend: Near the Texas coast, hazardous marine conditions with strong breezes and frequent gusts to 20 knots or higher will continue through Monday afternoon before gradually easing to a gentle to moderate southeasterly breeze Tuesday through the end of the week. Rain-free weather with highs in the 90s and heat indices of 100-108 is expected through Wednesday, followed by low rain chances mainly near the seabreeze Thursday and Friday. Hazy conditions from Saharan dust are possible at times today and Monday.

Forecast Summary

Updated December 30, 2025

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect marine conditions near the coast to improve as strong, gusty north winds diminish through Tuesday night. High pressure will settle in, leading to calmer offshore waters by midweek, with onshore winds returning later in the week. Close to the coast, a cold Canadian air mass will bring chilly temperatures, with lows in the upper 20s to lower 30s through Wednesday morning, and a light freeze possible both nights. Daytime highs will warm from the 50s on Tuesday to the 60s and low 70s by Thursday. No significant precipitation is expected in the near term.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect challenging marine conditions through Tuesday morning, with strong to gale-force north-northeasterly winds and elevated seas of 8-12 feet in the Gulf Waters. A Gale Warning is in effect until 6 PM Monday, followed by a Small Craft Advisory through Tuesday morning. Winds and seas will gradually decrease by Tuesday afternoon. Near the coast, cooler weather persists with highs around 60 on Monday and lows dropping into the 30s Tuesday night into Wednesday morning. No significant precipitation is expected in the immediate coastal areas.

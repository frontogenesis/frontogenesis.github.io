Updated May 05, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect mild marine conditions near the coast with onshore southerly winds of 10-15 knots through early Wednesday. Mostly dry weather is forecast close to the shore, though a few light rain showers are possible inland on Tuesday. A cold front will bring showers and thunderstorms Wednesday night into Thursday morning, shifting winds to offshore with stronger northeast winds of 15-20 knots by Thursday night into Friday. Warmer temperatures and increasing humidity are anticipated today and Wednesday, with a chance for more rain over the weekend.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect moderate to strong southeast winds along the coast through Tuesday, with winds briefly reaching advisory levels Tuesday afternoon for bays and nearshore waters south of Port Aransas. Marine conditions will feature a moderate to fresh onshore flow during this time. A cold front arriving Thursday will bring a chance of showers and thunderstorms later in the week, with winds shifting to a moderate to fresh northeast flow behind the front. Temperatures will be above normal Tuesday, with highs potentially reaching 100 inland, but will cool to the 70s and 80s Thursday and Friday after the front passes.

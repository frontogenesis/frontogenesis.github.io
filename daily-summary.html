Forecast Summary

Updated January 29, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect calm marine conditions today near the coast with light winds and low seas of 1-2 feet. However, a cold front will move through tonight into early Friday, bringing a few showers and storms, followed by strong, gusty winds and rough seas building to 4-7 feet by Friday afternoon, and potentially 8-10 feet in outer waters by Saturday. Conditions should improve late Saturday into Sunday. Onshore, temperatures will briefly warm to the mid-50s to mid-60s today before dropping significantly with the cold front, leading to well-below-normal temperatures and hard freeze conditions over the weekend.

Texas Coastal Bend: A cold front will move through the Texas Coastal Bend tonight, bringing frigid weather to the area. Near the coast, expect marine conditions to shift from light to strong breezes with gusts potentially reaching gale force as the front passes. These strong winds are expected to continue through Saturday before calming to a moderate breeze by Saturday night. There’s a low to medium chance (20-50%) of rain along the coast with the front’s arrival. Temperatures will drop significantly, with highs only in the 40s and 50s this weekend and lows in the 20s and 30s.

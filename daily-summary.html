Forecast Summary

Updated March 11, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, especially near the coast, expect challenging marine conditions starting Wednesday afternoon into Thursday. Scattered showers and thunderstorms, some potentially severe, are forecast for the afternoon and evening, with strong winds and rapidly building seas following a passing front. A Small Craft Advisory will be in effect from midnight Wednesday through Thursday evening, and there’s a chance of occasional gale-force gusts until noon Thursday. Onshore areas near the coast will also see these storms, with cooler temperatures in the mid-60s on Thursday, warming to the 70s by Friday.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect changing marine and weather conditions over the next few days. Today, marine conditions will be relatively calm with south winds and 4-5 ft seas, though isolated showers or thunderstorms are possible (30-50% chance). Overnight, a cold front will bring strong to near-gale northerly winds and rough seas of 7-10 ft by Thursday morning, likely prompting a Small Craft Advisory. Bays will also be rough. By Thursday night into Friday, winds will ease to light and seas will drop to 2-3 ft. Near the coast, isolated morning showers are expected today, followed by drier and cooler weather Thursday after the front passes. Temperatures will warm significantly by the weekend, reaching the upper 90s on Sunday, before another cold front arrives late Sunday, cooling things down again early next week.

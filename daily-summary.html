Forecast Summary

Updated December 28, 2025

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect changing weather conditions near the coast over the next few days. A cold front arriving late Sunday night into Monday morning will bring strong, gusty north winds, with sustained speeds of 25-35 knots and gusts up to 40 knots in marine areas. A Gale Watch is in effect for coastal waters from late Sunday night through Tuesday, with rough seas building to 3-6 feet nearshore and 6-8 feet farther offshore. Some scattered rain is possible with the front, but severe storms are not expected. Temperatures will start mild on Sunday but drop significantly after the front, with chilly conditions and lows in the 30s by Monday night and Tuesday, though areas right along the coast may stay slightly warmer. Winds and seas should begin to calm down by Tuesday as high pressure builds in.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect above-normal temperatures today with highs in the 80s, but a strong cold front arriving late Sunday night will bring much cooler weather, with highs dropping to the 50s early in the week and lows in the 30s by Tuesday night. Along the coast and in marine areas, anticipate a moderate chance of rain with the front, and be prepared for strong winds, with sustained speeds of 20-30 mph and gusts up to 45 mph, especially over the Gulf waters. A Gale Warning is in effect for the Gulf from early Monday morning through Monday evening, with seas building up to 15 feet offshore, increasing the risk of minor coastal flooding. Winds and seas should calm by midweek.

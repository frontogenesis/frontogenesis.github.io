Forecast Summary

Updated April 19, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect challenging marine conditions near the coast through Sunday, with strong northerly winds reaching gale force in the outer Gulf waters west of Intracoastal City and hazardous conditions for small craft across all marine zones into the evening. Winds will ease and shift to easterly or southeasterly by Monday night, calming to 10-15 knots for much of the week. Close to the coast, cooler temperatures in the low to mid-70s are forecast through Tuesday, warming to the low 80s by Thursday. Isolated to scattered rain chances will increase from mid-week into the weekend as disturbances bring moisture to the area.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect challenging marine conditions in the coming days with a gale warning in effect for offshore waters north of Port Aransas, where near gale force winds and gusts are anticipated through the night. Strong winds are also possible over other Middle Texas coastal waters, with small craft advisories in place. Closer to the coast, showers and a few thunderstorms are likely through Tuesday, with rain chances increasing again from Sunday into early next week. Temperatures will be well below normal, with highs in the 60s on Sunday and ranging from the 60s to lower 70s on Monday, before gradually warming later in the week.

Forecast Summary

Updated November 07, 2025

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect calm marine conditions near the coast through Saturday night, but a cold front will bring strong winds with sustained speeds around 25 knots and gusts up to 30 knots starting Sunday. This will cause rough seas with waves of 4 to 7 feet, and a Small Craft Advisory will likely be in effect Sunday through Monday. On land near the coast, temperatures will remain warm with highs in the mid-80s through Saturday, but a sharp drop is expected by Monday with highs only in the 50s. Rainfall will be minimal over the next week.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect warm weather heading into the weekend, with a strong cold front arriving Sunday morning. Near the coast, temperatures will drop to the 40s and 50s by Monday night into Tuesday morning. Marine conditions will be rough, with strong to near gale-force winds (sustained over 20 knots and gusts possibly exceeding 25 knots) and seas building to 10-15 feet from Sunday through Monday morning. There’s also a low chance of showers and thunderstorms along the front on Sunday. Conditions should improve by late Monday afternoon, with calmer winds and seas. There’s a slight chance of minor coastal flooding this weekend, though confidence in this is low.

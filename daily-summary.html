Forecast Summary

Updated December 01, 2025

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect challenging marine conditions along the coast this week. A Small Craft Advisory is in effect for coastal waters due to strong winds, with north winds around 20 knots and gusts up to 25 knots expected late Monday night into Tuesday afternoon. Wave heights will range from 5 to 6 feet in outer waters and 2 to 4 feet closer to shore. Additionally, a low water advisory may be needed Tuesday due to very low tides. Onshore, heavy rainfall is possible Monday through Monday night, with 1 to 3 inches expected and locally higher amounts. Temperatures will be cold, with lows near freezing north of I-10 Tuesday night and mid-30s along the coast.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect changing marine and weather conditions this week. Near the coast, light showers or drizzle are possible this morning, with rain chances decreasing by afternoon but returning Wednesday night through Friday. Temperatures will be below normal through Tuesday, with highs in the 50s today and lows in the mid-40s tonight along the coast, warming to the mid-60s to lower 70s by Wednesday. Strong northerly winds are forecast over the water through this morning and again late tonight into midday Tuesday, with gusty conditions possible. Winds will ease to a gentle to moderate onshore breeze Tuesday night through early Thursday, before another cold front brings stronger northerly flow by the weekend.

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect light onshore winds and calm seas through Sunday, with a chance of showers or thunderstorms near the coast in the afternoon. A weak cold front will bring more showers and thunderstorms late Sunday night into Monday, followed by breezy offshore winds into early Tuesday. Temperatures will be slightly cooler with lower humidity Monday night into Wednesday, before warming up again by the end of the week. Drier conditions are likely to persist through most of the week.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect light to gentle southeasterly breezes along the coast on Sunday, with a slight increase in winds and seas by afternoon and evening, particularly offshore. There’s a moderate chance of showers and thunderstorms late Sunday into early Monday as a cold front moves through, and a few storms could become strong to severe, bringing locally higher winds and seas. Winds will shift to northeasterly and strengthen briefly to moderate levels Monday morning before calming by Monday night. Temperatures will be hot on Sunday, with highs in the upper 80s to upper 90s, cooling to the low to mid-80s east of the coast by Monday and Tuesday. Light to moderate rainfall is expected with the front, generally under an inch, though isolated higher amounts are possible.

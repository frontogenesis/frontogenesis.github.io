Forecast Summary

Updated October 27, 2025

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect calm marine conditions near the coast with light winds and low seas through Tuesday. However, a cold front arriving Tuesday evening into Wednesday morning will bring much stronger winds, with sustained speeds and gusts potentially reaching Small Craft Advisory or Gale levels from Wednesday into Thursday. Close to the coast, the weather will start warm with highs in the low to mid-80s on Monday, but cooler temperatures will follow the front, dropping to the upper 40s to mid-50s by Wednesday morning and only reaching the mid to upper 60s in the afternoons. Scattered showers and storms are possible Tuesday into Wednesday, though no severe weather or flooding is expected.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect warm and dry weather to start the week, with highs in the upper 80s to mid-90s through Tuesday. Marine conditions will feature light to moderate onshore winds today, strengthening to moderate to fresh on Tuesday. A strong cold front arrives Tuesday night, bringing much cooler temperatures into the 50s by Wednesday morning and highs only in the low to mid-70s. Strong north-northwest winds are expected near the coast and over Gulf waters Tuesday night through Wednesday night, potentially nearing gale force, with a Small Craft Advisory likely and possible Gale headlines. Winds will gradually ease by Thursday into the weekend. Confidence is low for measurable rain with this front, and most areas are expected to stay dry.

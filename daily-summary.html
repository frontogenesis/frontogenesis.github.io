Updated March 16, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect challenging marine conditions near the coast through Monday night. A strong cold front will bring periodic gale force winds with gusts up to 40 knots over the coastal waters, along with rough seas. These strong northerly winds will continue into Monday night before gradually easing by midweek. Additionally, brief showers and storms are possible tonight near the coast as the front moves through. Temperatures will turn cooler after the front passes, but no significant precipitation is expected beyond tonight.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect challenging marine conditions today with strong gale-force northerly winds and very rough seas of 11-15 feet through the morning. These winds will decrease in the afternoon, but a Small Craft Advisory will remain in effect into the evening, with calmer seas expected by Tuesday morning. Near the coast, cooler temperatures will prevail today, nearly 30 degrees colder than yesterday, and this chill will persist through Wednesday morning. From Thursday onward, temperatures are expected to rise above normal through the weekend.

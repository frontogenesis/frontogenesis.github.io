Forecast Summary

Updated July 22, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: Tropical Storm Bertha is forecast to move westward near or just inland of the southwest Louisiana coast through Thursday, remaining asymmetrical with most impacts south of its center. Marine conditions will feature modest west winds today before increasing tonight, with tropical storm-force winds possible late Wednesday night into Thursday east of Cameron; all interests should monitor updates as conditions Thursday depend on the storm's track and strength. Isolated heavy rain and a 1-3 foot storm surge are possible in the coastal watch area. Extreme heat is expected today with heat indices of 112-118°F, followed by hot conditions and limited rain chances over the weekend.

Texas Coastal Bend: A gentle to moderate southerly wind will continue through the week over Coastal Bend waters, with occasional fresh southerly winds possible south of Port Aransas. Seas will build with increasing swell late in the week as Tropical Storm Bertha moves slowly westward across the northern Gulf, bringing a chance of isolated to scattered showers and thunderstorms Thursday evening into early Friday. Significant uncertainty remains regarding the storm's exact track and strength. Near the coast, highs in the upper 90s are expected with heat indices of 110-113 Wednesday and Thursday. Higher surf and an elevated rip current risk are likely this weekend.

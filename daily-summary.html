Forecast Summary

Updated February 18, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect mild marine conditions near the coast through the week with light to moderate onshore winds. There’s a chance of sea fog during the night and morning hours, particularly increasing by Thursday night. A cold front will arrive on Saturday, bringing a chance of showers and thunderstorms, followed by strong, gusty northerly winds by Sunday. Temperatures will remain warm, nearing record highs around 80 degrees on Thursday, before cooling significantly early next week.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect mild and mostly dry weather near the coast through the end of the week, with high temperatures in the 80s and possibly reaching 90 inland. Marine conditions will feature gentle to moderate onshore winds, with occasionally stronger breezes. Over the weekend, a cold front will bring a slight chance (15-20%) of showers along the coast and over area waters, along with stronger north-northeast winds and rough seas of 6-10 feet. This may lead to Small Craft Advisories, and there’s a possibility of near gale-force gusts offshore. Temperatures will cool slightly after the front, with lows in the 40s in northern areas early next week.

Forecast Summary

Updated January 30, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect challenging marine conditions near the coast through the weekend. A cold front will bring strong, gusty winds of 20-30 knots starting early Friday morning, lasting through Saturday night, with seas building to 4-10 feet. A Small Craft Advisory will be in effect during this period. Temperatures will be well below normal, with hard freezes expected over the weekend and lows in the teens and 20s. Wind chills will make it feel even colder, especially Saturday morning. Conditions should improve late Saturday into Sunday as winds lighten, and a warming trend begins by Sunday evening, though another freeze is possible Monday morning away from the coast.

Texas Coastal Bend: A strong cold front is moving through the Texas Coastal Bend, bringing chilly temperatures and strong winds to the coastal and marine areas. Expect north winds with gusts near gale force along the coast and offshore through Saturday, with a low chance of showers mainly offshore overnight. Winds will ease by Sunday, shifting to a weak to moderate onshore flow by Monday. Temperatures near the coast will drop, with highs in the 50s on Friday and lows in the 30s Friday night, before a gradual warming trend starts Sunday. It will remain mostly dry for now, though rain chances may return next week.

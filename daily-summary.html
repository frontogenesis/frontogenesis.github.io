Forecast Summary

Updated March 27, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect light onshore winds and calm seas along the coast through Friday. A dry cold front will move through late Friday night into Saturday morning, bringing stronger offshore winds of 20-25 knots and seas of 4 to 6 feet, likely requiring a small craft advisory. These conditions may also cause low water levels near the coast. Winds will ease by Saturday night and shift back onshore by Sunday. Temperatures will briefly cool on Saturday before warming up again next week, with small chances of rain returning later in the week.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect gentle to moderate south to southeast winds over the bays and nearshore waters today, with a fresh breeze at times south of Port Aransas. A cold front arriving tonight will shift winds to the northeast by Saturday, strengthening to a fresh breeze, and Small Craft Advisory conditions are possible. Winds will ease back to a gentle to moderate east to southeast flow by Saturday night. There’s a slight chance (20-50%) of showers over the coastal waters late Friday night into Saturday morning. Near the coast, temperatures will drop to near normal levels for the weekend following the front, though warmer than usual conditions are expected to return early next week. Most areas will remain dry with no significant rainfall anticipated.

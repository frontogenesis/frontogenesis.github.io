Forecast Summary

Updated April 17, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect warm and humid weather near the coast through Saturday afternoon with highs in the mid to upper 80s. A cold front will bring showers and thunderstorms Saturday evening into early Sunday, with potential for heavy rainfall in some areas. Marine conditions will become hazardous over the coastal waters starting Saturday night through Monday, with strong northerly winds and possible gale force gusts (over 34 knots) early Sunday morning, especially west of Cameron. A Gale Watch or Warning may be issued. Cooler, drier air will settle in Sunday through Tuesday, with temperatures dropping below normal before warming up again mid-week.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect changing marine and weather conditions over the coming days. On Friday, moderate to strong onshore winds will affect the bays and nearshore waters, especially south of Port Aransas, with a small craft advisory in effect into the night. A strong cold front arrives Saturday night, bringing very strong northeasterly winds along the coast and into open waters through Sunday. Showers and a few thunderstorms are likely from Saturday night into early next week, with temperatures cooling sharply to the 70s on Sunday and Monday. Rainfall could accumulate over half an inch on average, with some areas seeing up to 2 inches by Tuesday, though significant flooding is not expected. Winds will ease early next week, shifting to easterly and southeasterly directions.

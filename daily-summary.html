Forecast Summary

Updated December 17, 2025

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect changing weather near the coast over the next few days. Marine conditions will feature light to moderate easterly winds through early Thursday, shifting to stronger offshore winds by Friday morning, which may require a Small Craft Advisory due to gusty conditions. Onshore flow returns by late Friday, with a likelihood of marine fog developing over the weekend due to calm conditions and cool shelf waters meeting warm, moist air. Close to the coast, anticipate periods of showers through Thursday with above-normal temperatures in the mid-70s, cooling to more seasonal levels by Friday after a cold front passes, before warming again into the mid-to-upper 70s starting Saturday.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect wet weather today with rainfall amounts of 1-2 inches along the coast, and some areas could see heavy downpours from thunderstorms at rates of 1-3 inches per hour. Marine conditions will feature gentle to moderate onshore winds and seas of 2-3 feet through Thursday afternoon, increasing to 4-6 feet with stronger winds after a cold front passes Thursday night. Coastal showers and thunderstorms are likely today but should clear by Thursday morning. Temperatures will drop to more seasonal levels by Friday, with dry conditions expected through the weekend, followed by above-average warmth into Christmas week.

Forecast Summary

Updated January 11, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: For Southwest Louisiana, expect challenging marine conditions near the coast through late Monday night, with strong, gusty offshore winds and seas ranging from 3 to 8 feet. Low water conditions are also anticipated in Sabine and Calcasieu lakes early Sunday morning due to the combination of these winds and low tide. Close to the coast, breezy northerly winds will keep temperatures feeling quite cold, with apparent temperatures in the 30s on Sunday morning. Overall, temperatures will remain below seasonal averages through the week, with highs in the mid-50s on Monday and another cold front arriving Wednesday night, bringing minimal precipitation.

Texas Coastal Bend: For the Texas Coastal Bend, expect improving marine conditions near the coast as strong winds and high seas diminish through Sunday morning into early afternoon, though a Small Craft Advisory remains in effect until then. By Sunday night, winds will ease to a gentle to moderate breeze. There’s a slight chance (20-30%) of showers through Sunday morning and again from Monday night into Wednesday. Temperatures will stay cool with highs in the 60s early in the week, warming to the 70s by Wednesday, while lows remain in the 40s. There’s also a low chance (20-40%) of rain through Tuesday.

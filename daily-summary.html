Forecast Summary

Updated June 18, 2026

Southwest Louisiana: Remnants of post-Tropical Storm Arthur have moved inland, canceling all tropical storm warnings for Southwest Louisiana. Strong squalls continue offshore with sustained winds of 15-25 knots and waves up to 10 feet, creating dangerous marine conditions that will gradually improve Thursday night as winds drop below 20 knots; waves may remain above 6 feet into Friday before calming to 10-15 knots and under 5 feet over the weekend. A coastal flood advisory remains in effect through Thursday afternoon with surges up to 2 feet, while a flood watch continues through Thursday evening amid an additional 2-6 inches of rain possible overnight and into Thursday morning. Highs will reach the 90s Thursday with high humidity, followed by more typical mid-90s summertime conditions and low rain chances below 20% through the weekend.

Texas Coastal Bend: South to southeast winds of moderate to fresh strength will continue across the coastal waters into early next week, with occasional stronger gusts possible in bays and nearshore areas during the afternoon; small craft advisories may be needed at times. Near the coast, highs will reach the mid-90s under a heat advisory today, producing dangerous heat indices of 110-114. Rain chances remain low today but increase over the weekend. The rip current risk stays low through tonight before becoming moderate on Friday, when minor coastal flooding is also possible during high tide.
